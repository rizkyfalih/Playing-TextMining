VW unveils new Passat, says sales well 

Germany's Volkswagen AG , unveiling a new Passat car aimed at boosting its share of the mid-size market, said on Wednesday its total sales for the first seven months of 1996 were up in Europe and the world.

The Wolfsburg-based carmaker said worldwide deliveries to customers from January to July were up 13.5 percent to 2.35 million vehicles, while deliveries in western Europe were up 11.3 percent to 1.451 million vehicles

But the German market for Europe's largest carmaker was still anaemic, with growth at only 2.8 percent in the period.
